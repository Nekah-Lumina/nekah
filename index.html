The Problem

Maternal and newborn health outcomes are still affected by delayed symptom recognition, inconsistent support, and gaps in care continuity, especially in underserved communities.

⚕️

Delayed risk recognition

Early symptoms often go unnoticed or are misunderstood, allowing preventable complications during pregnancy and postpartum care to worsen before help is reached.

💗

Limited maternal support

Many mothers navigate pregnancy and early motherhood without continuous guidance, emotional reassurance, or tools that help them understand when they need care.

Impact for Mothers and Children

NEKAH is designed to help mothers recognize health risks earlier, access more reliable guidance, and feel supported throughout pregnancy and early motherhood.

Why it matters

By combining intelligent technology with maternal health awareness, NEKAH aims to reduce preventable complications during pregnancy and postpartum care while supporting stronger maternal wellbeing.

Who it helps

NEKAH is especially relevant for mothers in underserved communities, first-time mothers, and young mothers who may need earlier guidance, emotional reassurance, and stronger healthcare support pathways.

Earlier Action Helps mothers know when care may be needed sooner.
Better Wellbeing Encourages guidance, reassurance, and emotional support.
Healthier Outcomes Seeks to improve continuity of care for mothers and babies.

Founder

NEKAH is founded and led by a mission-driven innovator focused on improving maternal and newborn outcomes through technology.

Francisca-Gina Anurika Umoh

Francisca-Gina Anurika Umoh is the founder of NEKAH and NekahLumina Ventures. She created NEKAH after identifying critical gaps in maternal healthcare support, particularly around early symptom detection, emergency response, and emotional support for mothers.

She won First Place in the 2025 HelpMum CareCode Hackathon, where NEKAH received recognition as an innovative maternal health solution and was awarded a ₦2,000,000 grant and mentorship support.
